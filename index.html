Acoustic Analysis for Green Mosque Dzarratul Muthmainnah

This project involved conducting comprehensive acoustic analysis for the renovation of Dzarratul Muthmainnah Green Mosque in South Tangerang. The work focused on optimizing the sound system and improving room acoustics to enhance the worship experience.

We assessed the existing conditions through detailed measurements and simulations, then provided recommendations for sound system upgrades including speaker placement optimization and acoustic treatment options for the prayer hall.

The project aimed to create a serene and acoustically pleasant environment for the congregation, ensuring that speech intelligibility and sound quality are maintained during prayers and religious events.

Through this project, we demonstrated the importance of acoustics in religious spaces and how proper acoustic design can significantly impact the spiritual experience of worshippers.

Parametric Study on Plenum Windows

This research focused on evaluating the acoustic performance of plenum windows using advanced simulation techniques including Finite-Difference Time-Domain (FDTD) and Finite-Element Method analyses.

We conducted a comprehensive parametric study examining variations in glass separation, overlapping dimensions, pane angles, and the addition of sonic crystals between panes to optimize acoustic performance.

The findings provide valuable insights into the design of energy-efficient windows that can significantly reduce urban noise pollution while maintaining architectural appeal and natural ventilation capabilities.

This project contributes to the development of innovative building envelope solutions that enhance both acoustic comfort and sustainability in urban environments.

Transparent Wood Research

This project explores the potential of transparent wood as a revolutionary building material that combines aesthetic appeal with functional performance in passive cooling systems.

We investigated its optical, thermal, and mechanical properties, along with potential applications in building facades and fenestration systems aimed at reducing energy consumption.

The research highlights the unique benefits of using transparent wood in architecture, including its ability to provide natural daylighting while maintaining thermal comfort and structural integrity.

Our findings suggest that transparent wood could play a significant role in the future of sustainable building practices, particularly in tropical climates where balancing daylight and heat gain is crucial.

Energy Efficiency Roadmap for Indonesia

This nationwide project involved surveying and modeling baseline energy consumption patterns across various building typologies in multiple Indonesian cities.

We formulated a comprehensive set of energy efficiency measures targeting architectural design, building envelope optimization, HVAC systems, and occupant behavior to achieve significant energy savings.

The developed roadmap serves as a practical guideline for policymakers, developers, and building professionals to implement cost-effective energy-saving strategies in Indonesia's rapidly growing building sector.

This initiative represents a crucial step toward promoting sustainable urban development and addressing climate change challenges through improved building energy performance.
