FULL PROFILE

Laurentius Kevin Hendinata is an engineer specializing in the intersection of building physics and sustainable energy, with particular expertise in acoustic performance of building openings. The research work encompasses both theoretical modeling and practical applications, supported by multiple academic awards and grants.

A Bachelor of Engineering degree in Engineering Physics was completed at Universitas Gadjah Mada, where recognition as the Faculty of Engineering's Most Outstanding Student was received. Currently, a joint master's degree in acoustic engineering is being pursued through the prestigious Erasmus Mundus WAVES program, spanning three European universities.

Collaboration opportunities are welcomed in the fields of architectural acoustics, building physics, and energy-efficient design.

Acoustic Analysis for Green Mosque Dzarratul Muthmainnah

This project provided comprehensive acoustic consultation for the renovation of the Dzarratul Muthmainnah Green Mosque in South Tangerang. The primary focus was optimizing interior acoustics through material selection and sound system design.

Key recommendations included:

  • Strategic placement of sound-absorbing materials in the prayer hall to control reverberation
  • Selection of surface finishes that balance aesthetic requirements with acoustic performance
  • Specialized acoustic treatment for the podcast recording space to ensure speech clarity
  • Custom design of the public address system for optimal speech intelligibility

The implementation resulted in significantly improved worship experience with clear audio throughout the prayer spaces and professional-quality recording capabilities in the podcast studio.

Parametric Study on Plenum Windows

This investigation systematically evaluated the acoustic performance of plenum window designs through advanced numerical modeling techniques. Finite-Difference Time-Domain (FDTD) and Finite-Element Method analyses were employed to assess sound transmission characteristics under various geometric configurations.

Key parameters including glass separation distance, pane overlap dimensions, angular orientations, and the integration of sonic crystal arrays were examined. The study revealed non-linear relationships between geometric modifications and acoustic performance, with particular sensitivity observed in the mid-frequency range.

Practical design guidelines were developed from the findings, enabling architects to optimize window configurations for both noise reduction and natural ventilation requirements.

Transparent Wood Research

This materials research initiative explored the potential of transparent wood composites as multifunctional building elements. Optical, thermal, and mechanical properties were characterized through laboratory testing and computational modeling, with particular attention to their performance in tropical climates.

The investigation demonstrated how lignin-modified wood substrates could simultaneously provide daylight transmission, thermal insulation, and structural capacity. Comparative analyses with conventional glazing systems revealed significant advantages in terms of embodied energy and passive cooling potential.

Prototype facade elements were developed and monitored under real-world conditions, validating simulation predictions about their ability to reduce cooling loads while maintaining visual connectivity with the exterior environment.

Energy Efficiency Roadmap

This nationwide study established comprehensive energy consumption benchmarks for diverse building typologies across Indonesia's major urban centers. Data was collected through systematic audits of hundreds of buildings, complemented by utility record analysis and occupant surveys.

Statistical models were developed to identify key drivers of energy use intensity, accounting for climatic variations, operational patterns, and building characteristics. The analysis revealed significant opportunities for improvement, particularly in cooling systems and lighting efficiency.

A tiered implementation roadmap was formulated, prioritizing cost-effective measures with rapid payback periods alongside more comprehensive retrofitting strategies.

Green Building Certification

This project involved comprehensive energy modeling and performance analysis to support green building certification for the UGM Innovation and Creativity Center. Both static calculations and dynamic thermal simulations were conducted to evaluate the building's energy consumption patterns and indoor environmental quality.

The assessment covered multiple criteria including energy efficiency, water conservation, indoor air quality, and material sustainability. Innovative features such as hybrid ventilation strategies and responsive shading systems were analyzed through computational fluid dynamics and daylight modeling.

The implementation of these measures resulted in a 40% reduction in predicted energy use compared to conventional designs, qualifying the project for Gold-level certification under Indonesia's Green Building rating system.

Smart Windows Technology

This comprehensive review analyzed active and passive smart window technologies with potential applications in tropical climates. Various technologies were evaluated including electrochromic, thermochromic, photochromic, and suspended particle device systems.

Performance characteristics such as optical modulation range, switching speed, durability, and energy efficiency were compared across different technologies. Special attention was given to their suitability for Indonesian building conditions, considering factors like high humidity, intense solar radiation, and typical usage patterns.

The findings provide architects and building designers with practical guidance for selecting appropriate smart window technologies based on specific project requirements and environmental conditions.

Coiled-Up Silencer Tubes

This project focused on developing innovative noise insulation solutions for urban environments. The design of coiled-up silencer tubes was explored to effectively reduce sound transmission in high-density areas.

Through computational modeling and experimental validation, the acoustic performance of these tubes was assessed under various conditions. The results indicated significant reductions in noise levels, making them suitable for applications in residential and commercial buildings.

Field tests demonstrated their effectiveness in mitigating noise pollution, contributing to improved urban living conditions.

Solar Street Lighting

This project aimed to implement renewable energy solutions through solar street lighting systems in urban areas. The design focused on maximizing energy efficiency and minimizing environmental impact.

A comprehensive analysis of solar panel efficiency, battery storage, and LED technology was conducted to ensure optimal performance. The project included pilot installations that demonstrated significant energy savings and enhanced public safety.

Feedback from community stakeholders highlighted the positive impact on nighttime visibility and overall urban aesthetics.

IoT Mushroom Cultivation

This project explored the integration of Internet of Things (IoT) technology in mushroom cultivation to enhance environmental monitoring and optimize growth conditions.

Sensors were deployed to track temperature, humidity, and CO2 levels, providing real-time data to farmers. The system enabled automated adjustments to the growing environment, resulting in increased yield and reduced resource consumption.

Data analytics were utilized to refine cultivation practices, leading to improved sustainability in agricultural operations.

Environmental Literacy Study

This study assessed the level of environmental awareness among various demographics in Indonesia, focusing on energy conservation practices and sustainability education.

Surveys and interviews were conducted to gather data on knowledge, attitudes, and behaviors related to energy use. The findings revealed significant gaps in understanding and highlighted the need for targeted educational initiatives.

Recommendations were made to enhance environmental literacy through community programs and school curricula, aiming to foster a culture of sustainability.
