FULL PROFILE

Laurentius Kevin Hendinata is an engineer specializing in the intersection of building physics and sustainable energy, with particular expertise in acoustic performance of building openings. The research work encompasses both theoretical modeling and practical applications, supported by multiple academic awards and grants.

A Bachelor of Engineering degree in Engineering Physics was completed at Universitas Gadjah Mada, where recognition as the Faculty of Engineering's Most Outstanding Student was received. Currently, a joint master's degree in acoustic engineering is being pursued through the prestigious Erasmus Mundus WAVES program, spanning three European universities.

Collaboration opportunities are welcomed in the fields of architectural acoustics, building physics, and energy-efficient design.

Decoupled CLT Junctions’ Insulation Parameters for Flanking Sound Transmission

This project involved the development of a Kij measurement setup and FEM modeling to analyze sound transmission through decoupled CLT junctions. The study focused on resilient interlayers and angle brackets for effective insulation.

Key aspects included:

  • 2D plane-strain model for solid mechanics analysis
  • Vibrational energy and transmission analysis
  • Referring to EN ISO 10848 and EN ISO 12354 standards
×

Sound Transmission through Plenum Windows with Variable Geometries

This research project focused on the sound transmission characteristics of plenum windows with variable geometries. Finite-Difference Time-Domain (FDTD) and Finite-Element Method (FEM) simulations were employed to analyze acoustic performance.

Key parameters studied included:

  • Glass separation
  • Overlapping dimensions
  • Pane angle
  • Inclusion of sonic crystals

The findings are currently under review for publication in Glass Structures & Engineering.

Integrated Dynamic Energy and FEM Acoustic Model of Fixed Window Overhangs

This project integrated dynamic energy analysis with FEM acoustic modeling to evaluate the performance of fixed window overhangs. The analysis was conducted using DesignBuilder for energy dynamics and COMSOL for acoustic-structure interaction.

Key results included:

  • 14.23% reduction in HVAC load
  • -3 to +12 dB insertion loss

The results are currently under review for publication in Building Simulations.

Non-Uniform Helmholtz Resonator Arrays for Broadband Sound Manipulation

This project analyzed the transfer matrix method for various configurations of Helmholtz resonators, including single, periodic identical, non-uniform, and periodic non-uniform HR.

Acoustic Impedance and Absorption Characteristics of Microperforated Panels

This project involved analytical impedance analysis for microperforated panels with various configurations, including circular, slit, and squared MPP.

The study focused on uniform and graded configurations, utilizing polynomial fitting on the parameters and resonance analysis from airgap backing.

Helmholtz Resonators Design for Perfect Absorption of Low Frequency Sound

This project focused on the analytical design and analysis of Helmholtz resonators for low-frequency sound absorption. The complex frequency plane analysis and pole-zero trajectories were utilized to optimize the design.

Experimental measurements were conducted using an impedance tube to validate the design.

Acoustic Consultancy for Green Mosque Dzarratul Muthmainnah

This project provided comprehensive acoustic consultancy for the renovation of the Dzarratul Muthmainnah Green Mosque in South Tangerang. The primary focus was optimizing interior acoustics through material selection and sound system design.

Key recommendations included:

  • Strategic placement of sound-absorbing materials in the prayer hall to control reverberation
  • Selection of surface finishes that balance aesthetic requirements with acoustic performance
  • Specialized acoustic treatment for the podcast recording space to ensure speech clarity
  • Custom design of the public address system for optimal speech intelligibility

The implementation resulted in significantly improved worship experience with clear audio throughout the prayer spaces and professional-quality recording capabilities in the podcast studio.

Coiled-Up Silencer for Outdoor Noise Insulation Applications

This project focused on developing innovative noise insulation solutions for urban environments. The design of coiled-up silencer tubes was explored to effectively reduce sound transmission in high-density areas.

Through computational modeling and experimental validation, the acoustic performance of these tubes was assessed under various conditions. The results indicated significant reductions in noise levels, making them suitable for applications in residential and commercial buildings.

Field tests demonstrated their effectiveness in mitigating noise pollution, contributing to improved urban living conditions.
