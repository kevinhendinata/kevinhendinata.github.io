Decoupled CLT Junctions' Insulation Parameters for Flanking Sound Transmission

This comprehensive research project focused on quantifying the Kij parameters for cross-laminated timber (CLT) junctions to predict and control flanking sound transmission in modern timber buildings. The study developed an innovative measurement setup capable of characterizing resilient interlayers and angle brackets with high precision.

Key technical aspects included:

  • Advanced 2D plane-strain finite element model in COMSOL Multiphysics to analyze vibrational energy transmission pathways
  • Experimental validation using laser Doppler vibrometry to measure structural vibration levels
  • Statistical energy analysis to predict sound transmission loss through complex junction systems
  • Development of parametric models considering variable stiffness and damping characteristics

The methodology strictly followed EN ISO 10848 and EN ISO 12354 standards for building acoustics measurements, ensuring the results are directly applicable in architectural acoustics practice. The developed framework provides architects and engineers with reliable prediction tools during the design phase of timber buildings.

Sound Transmission through Plenum Windows with Variable Geometries

This research systematically investigated the acoustic attenuation performance of plenum windows with various geometric configurations. The study employed both Finite-Difference Time-Domain (FDTD) and Finite-Element Method (FEM) approaches to thoroughly characterize the sound transmission loss characteristics.

The computational framework included:

  • MATLAB implementation of FDTD with impedance boundary conditions to simulate sound wave propagation
  • COMSOL Multiphysics acoustic-structure interaction models for detailed frequency-domain analysis
  • Parametric optimization considering glass separation distances (50-300mm), overlapping ratios (25-100%), pane angles (0-30Â°), and integrated sonic crystal arrays
  • Validation through laboratory measurements in an anechoic chamber with controlled broadband noise excitation

The results demonstrated that optimized plenum window configurations can achieve up to 12 dB improvement in sound reduction index compared to conventional double-glazed units. These findings are currently under peer-review for publication in Glass Structures & Engineering, with potential implications for urban noise mitigation strategies.

Integrated Dynamic Energy and FEM Acoustic Model of Fixed Window Overhangs

This interdisciplinary project developed a novel integrated modeling approach combining building energy dynamics with acoustic performance analysis for fixed window overhang systems. The research bridged the gap between architectural acoustics and sustainable design by simultaneously evaluating both energy efficiency and noise control metrics.

The methodology incorporated:

  • Dynamic thermal modeling in DesignBuilder to quantify HVAC load reductions under varying climatic conditions
  • Finite element acoustic analysis in COMSOL to predict insertion loss spectra (63Hz-4kHz frequency range)
  • Computational fluid dynamics simulations to assess airflow patterns affecting both thermal and acoustic performance
  • Multi-objective optimization considering solar heat gain coefficients, U-values, and sound transmission class ratings

The integrated model demonstrated that optimized overhang designs can achieve 14.23% annual HVAC load reduction while simultaneously providing 3-12 dB of broadband noise attenuation, depending on the specific geometric configuration and material properties. This research challenges the conventional trade-off between natural ventilation and noise control in urban buildings.

Non-Uniform Helmholtz Resonator Arrays for Broadband Sound Manipulation

This theoretical and experimental investigation developed an advanced transfer matrix methodology for analyzing non-uniform Helmholtz resonator (HR) arrays with applications in broadband sound absorption and wave manipulation. The research significantly extended conventional HR analysis by incorporating non-identical resonator elements and graded configurations.

Key innovations included:

  • Generalized transfer matrix formulation for systems with arbitrary resonator spacing and dimensional variations
  • Numerical implementation with variable neck lengths (5-50mm), cavity volumes (100-1000cmÂ³), and perforation ratios (0.5-5%)
  • Experimental validation using 3D-printed prototypes tested in impedance tube setups
  • Bandgap optimization algorithms combining genetic algorithms with finite element simulations

The resulting analytical framework enables precise prediction of acoustic impedance for complex HR arrays, facilitating the design of metamaterials with tailored frequency responses for architectural and industrial noise control applications.

Acoustic Impedance and Absorption Characteristics of Microperforated Panels

This comprehensive study systematically characterized the acoustical performance of microperforated panels (MPPs) with various geometries and backing configurations. The research advanced fundamental understanding of MPP behavior through analytical modeling, numerical simulation, and experimental validation across a broad parameter space.

Major contributions included:

  • Development of unified impedance models for circular, slit, and square perforation shapes
  • Implementation of graded configuration designs with linear, quadratic, and exponential parameter variations
  • Advanced resonance analysis incorporating variable airgap depths (10-300mm) and porous backing materials
  • Experimental validation through impedance tube measurements and comparison with theoretical predictions

The findings revealed that MPPs can achieve significant sound absorption coefficients across a wide frequency range, making them suitable for various architectural applications, including concert halls and recording studios. This research contributes to the development of sustainable acoustic solutions in building design.

Helmholtz Resonators Design for Perfect Absorption of Low Frequency Sound

This project focused on the analytical design and experimental validation of Helmholtz resonators specifically tailored for low-frequency sound absorption. The research aimed to optimize resonator parameters to achieve maximum absorption efficiency in the critical low-frequency range, which is often challenging in architectural acoustics.

Key aspects of the project included:

  • Complex frequency plane analysis to identify optimal pole-zero trajectories for resonator design
  • Experimental measurement techniques utilizing impedance tubes to validate theoretical predictions
  • Design of resonators with varying neck lengths and cavity volumes to assess performance across different frequencies
  • Application of advanced fitting techniques to refine absorption characteristics based on empirical data

The results indicated that the optimized Helmholtz resonators could achieve absorption coefficients exceeding 0.9 at targeted frequencies, providing effective solutions for controlling low-frequency noise in various environments, including auditoriums and open-plan offices.

Acoustic Consultancy for Green Mosque Dzarratul Muthmainnah

This project involved providing comprehensive acoustic consultancy services for the renovation of the Dzarratul Muthmainnah Green Mosque in South Tangerang. The primary objective was to enhance the acoustic environment within the mosque while ensuring sustainability and aesthetic integrity.

Key deliverables included:

  • Detailed sound system design, including optimal microphone and speaker placement to ensure uniform sound distribution
  • Room acoustic analysis to evaluate reverberation times and sound clarity, leading to recommendations for acoustic treatments
  • Selection of sound-absorbing materials that align with the mosque's architectural style and sustainability goals
  • Implementation of specialized acoustic treatments in the prayer hall and community spaces to enhance worship experience

The consultancy resulted in a significant improvement in acoustic quality, providing a serene environment conducive to worship and community gatherings, while also achieving energy efficiency through the use of sustainable materials.

Coiled-Up Silencer for Outdoor Noise Insulation Applications

This project focused on the design and development of innovative coiled-up silencers aimed at mitigating outdoor noise pollution in urban environments. The research explored the acoustic performance of these silencers in various configurations to optimize their effectiveness in reducing sound transmission.

Key components of the project included:

  • Finite Element Method (FEM) simulations to model sound propagation and absorption characteristics of coiled-up designs
  • Parametric studies assessing the impact of cell size, configuration, and material properties on noise reduction performance
  • Field measurements conducted at urban sites to validate simulation results and assess real-world effectiveness
  • Development of guidelines for integrating coiled-up silencers into urban planning and building design

The findings demonstrated that these silencers could achieve substantial noise reduction, making them a viable solution for enhancing urban living conditions by addressing outdoor noise challenges.
